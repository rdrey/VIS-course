1 SHADOWFEVER, by Karen Marie Moning. (Delacorte, $26.) Hunting for her sister's murderer, MacKayla Lane is caught up in the struggle between humans and the Fae.
    
2 STRATEGIC MOVES, by Stuart Woods. (Putnam, $25.95.) In the 19th Stone Barrington novel, the New York lawyer works with the C.I.A. to transport a fugitive. 

3 THE GIRL WHO KICKED THE HORNET'S NEST, by Stieg Larsson. (Knopf, $27.95.) The third volume of a trilogy about a Swedish hacker and a journalist.

4 THE INNER CIRCLE, by Brad Meltzer. (Grand Central, $26.99.) An archivist discovers a book that once belonged to George Washington and conceals a deadly secret. 

5 THE SENTRY, by Robert Crais. (Putnam, $26.95.) Joe Pike and Elvis Cole, Hollywood private eyes, try to help a woman who is not as innocent as she appears. 

6 CALL ME IRRESISTIBLE, by Susan Elizabeth Phillips. (William Morrow/HarperCollins, $25.99.) In a small town in Texas, characters from Phillips's earlier novels reappear as a woman persuades a friend to call off her wedding to the town's popular mayor.

7 DEAD OR ALIVE, by Tom Clancy with Grant Blackwood. (Putnam, $28.95.) Familiar Clancy characters appear as an intelligence group tracks a vicious terrorist called the Emir. 

8 THE HELP, by Kathryn Stockett. (Amy Einhorn/Putnam, $24.95.) A young white woman and two black maids in 1960s Mississippi. 

9 THE CONFESSION, by John Grisham. (Doubleday, $28.95.) A criminal wants to save an innocent man on death row, but he must convince the authorities he's telling the truth.

10 WHAT THE NIGHT KNOWS, by Dean Koontz. (Bantam, $28.) Someone is murdering entire families, recreating in detail a crime spree that took place two decades earlier. 

11 ROOM, by Emma Donoghue. (Little, Brown, $24.99.) A mother's prison is her young son's entire world.

12 CROSS FIRE, by James Patterson. (Little, Brown, $27.99.) Alex Cross investigates a professional assassination in Washington, and receives a chilling call from a deadly adversary. 

13 FREEDOM, by Jonathan Franzen. (Farrar, Straus & Giroux, $28.) A family of Midwestern liberals during the Bush years. 

14 THE OUTLAWS, by W. E. B. Griffin and William E. Butterworth IV. (Putnam, $27.95.) Charley Castillo investigates a deadly biohazard that he thought had been destroyed. 

15 THREE SECONDS, by Anders Roslund and Borge Hellstrom. (Silver Oak, $24.95.) An undercover police officer takes on the organized drug traffic in Scandinavian prisons.
