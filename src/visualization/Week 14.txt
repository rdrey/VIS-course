1 TOYS, by James Patterson and Neil McMahon. (Little, Brown, $27.99.) Hays Baker, a top operative for the Agency of Change and a national hero, suddenly finds himself a hunted fugitive who must fight to save humans from extinction. 
    
2 SING YOU HOME, by Jodi Picoult. (Atria, $28.) Picoult takes on the issue of gay rights in this novel about a music therapist who desperately wants a child; CD of songs included. 

3 THE JUNGLE, by Clive Cussler with Jack Du Brul. (Putnam, $27.95.) Juan Cabrillo and the crew of the Oregon undertake rescue operations from Afghanistan to Myanmar. 

4 THE GIRL WHO KICKED THE HORNET'S NEST, by Stieg Larsson. (Knopf, $27.95.) The third volume of the Millennium trilogy, about a Swedish hacker and a journalist.

5 THE WISE MAN'S FEAR, by Patrick Rothfuss. (DAW, $29.95.) In this sequel to 'The Name of the Wind,' the magician Kvothe continues to narrate his fantastical life story.

6 THE TIGER'S WIFE, by Tea Obreht. (Random House, $25.) In an unnamed Balkan country, Natalia, a young doctor, investigates the mysterious circumstances surrounding her beloved grandfather's death.

7 THE PARIS WIFE, by Paula McLain. (Ballantine, $25.) Ernest Hemingway's first wife, Hadley, narrates this novel set in Paris.

8 A DISCOVERY OF WITCHES, by Deborah Harkness. (Viking, $28.95.) The recovery of a lost ancient manuscript in a library at Oxford sets a fantastical underworld stirring. 

9 MINDING FRANKIE, by Maeve Binchy. (Knopf, $26.95.) A motherless girl is cared for by a Dublin community, but a social worker believes a foster home is a better solution. 

10 LOVE YOU MORE, by Lisa Gardner. (Bantam, $26.) Detective D. D. Warren must solve the case of a dead husband, a battered wife and a missing child. 

11 TICK TOCK, by James Patterson and Michael Ledwidge. (Little, Brown, $27.99.) The New York detective Michael Bennett enlists the help of a former colleague to solve a rash of horrifying crimes that are throwing the city into chaos. 

12 THE HELP, by Kathryn Stockett. (Amy Einhorn/Putnam, $24.95.) A young white woman and two black maids in 1960s Mississippi. 

13 RIVER MARKED, by Patricia Briggs. (Ace, $26.95.) The shapeshifter Mercy Thompson and her mate, the Alpha werewolf Adam, confront evil lurking in the Columbia River. 

14 TREACHERY IN DEATH, by J. D. Robb. (Putnam, $26.95.) Eve Dallas and her partner, Peabody, investigate a grocer's murder.

15 ROOM, by Emma Donoghue. (Little, Brown, $24.99.) The entire world of the 5-year-old boy who narrates this novel is the 11-by-11-foot room in which his mother is being held against her will.
